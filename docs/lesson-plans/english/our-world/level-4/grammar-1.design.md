## The lesson

- **Course / Level / Unit:** Our World 2e · Level 4 · Unit 8 · Grammar 1
- **Spread:** Student Book pp. 136-137 · TR 8.4 · LP pp. 268-271
- **Objective:** give information about people using phrases with *who*.
- **Pattern:** **PERSON + who + verb phrase** — *I have a brother **who** loves video games.*
- **Academic Language:** clause, contraction · **Content Vocabulary:** trilobite · **Recycled:** creative
- **Hard line — people only.** No *that* / *which* as relative pronouns. No who-vs-which/that sorting. Previous deck violated this; the new deck does not.

## The spine — FC Leo United

Leo is the manager of **FC Leo United**, building his Champions League dream squad. He starts with an empty tactical board: 11 player positions in a 4-3-3 + 5 support staff slots. **Each slot only fills when a *who*-clause is correctly produced or parsed.** No *who*-clause = no recruit. **Why this clears both bars (Detailed + Creative):**

- *Load-bearing:* Leo literally cannot recruit a slot without producing a *who*-clause. The grammar IS the unlock mechanism.
- *Accumulates:* the squad board fills slot by slot across the whole deck instead of resetting every slide.
- *Earned payoff:* the formative-check final slide reveals all 11 players on the pitch + 5 staff in the dugout, each captioned with the *who*-clause that recruited them.
- *Threads LP beats verbatim:* every Practice item, every Recap join, and the class survey are mechanically wired to slots — they are not decorative drills.

## No-leaps proofs (sentence transformations are watched, never cut)

The Present + Practice slides erase characters one at a time so the transformation is *visible*:

```
There is a boy in my class. He takes photos.
                              ^^         ^
                              |          period erased (tap 1)
                              He erased (tap 2)
                              ↓
There is a boy in my class    who takes photos.
                              ↑
                              who inserted (tap 3)
```

The middle-*who* slide does this with the wrong attempt first (red shake), then re-positions: *My friend **who** collects movie posters knows a lot about actors.*

## JS safety contract (locked, future grammar skills inherit)

- ONE `<script>` block at the bottom of the file. Multiple tags break function references.
- All sentence content in **backtick template strings** so apostrophes (*it's, they're, who's*) are safe.
- **Never escape apostrophes inside `onclick`.** Use `data-text` attribute + helper.
- **No `confirm()` anywhere.** Two-tap "armed" pattern for destructive actions.
- Any badge / progress update wrapped in `try/catch` with a null check on the element.
- `node -e "$(awk '/<script>/{f=1;next}/<\/script>/{f=0}f' …)"` must parse cleanly before commit.
