## The lesson

- **Course / Level / Unit:** Our World 2e · Level 4 · Unit 8 · Grammar 2
- **Spread:** Student Book pp. 138-139 · TR 8.6 · LP pp. 274-275
- **Objective:** identify and use **direct and indirect objects** in a sentence.
- **Pattern A:** `subject + verb + DIRECT OBJECT + to/for + INDIRECT OBJECT` — *My dad gave **this fossil** **to me**.*
- **Pattern B:** `subject + verb + INDIRECT OBJECT + DIRECT OBJECT` — *My dad gave **me** **this fossil**.*
- **Same meaning** — two equivalent shapes.
- **Verbs in scope (with *to*):** give, show, send, tell, write.
- **Verbs in scope (with *for*):** buy, make.
- **Academic Language:** direct object, indirect object · **Content Vocabulary:** none new · **Recycled:** all 5 V2 collecting words land as direct objects (fossil, comic book, stuffed animal, bug, dinosaur), plus *who* from G1 in extended frames.
- **Hard scope line.** ONLY the 7 verbs above. No passive voice. No preview of object pronouns Leo hasn't seen. No introduction of *that*-clauses, no relative pronouns. Two patterns + the to/for choice — full stop.

## The spine — Trophy Day at the Bernabéu

Real Madrid have just won the Champions League Final 2-0 over Liverpool at the Bernabéu. The trophy-day celebration unfolds across **8 give/show/send/buy moments** between players, coaches, fans, sponsors and ballboys. Each spine slot fills ONLY when Leo produces or parses a correct double-object sentence (in either pattern). By the formative-check final slide the whole ceremony reads as 8 captioned photos.

- *Load-bearing:* the spine slot only fills when a correct double-object sentence is produced or parsed. No sentence → no moment unlocked.

- *Earned payoff:* slide 39 reveals the full Trophy Day photo with all 8 sentence captions on the celebration scene.
- *Threads LP beats verbatim (activity-shape level):* every LP Practice item, the Apply game-board mechanic with the coin token + me/him/her/them rule, and the Extend "3-gifts" list are all preserved as activities; only the surface story is soccer.
- *Teaching-first:* every slide carries 3-5 sample sentences (per the density mandate). The spine wraps the teaching; the teaching is never sacrificed for spine drama.

## No-leaps proof — the pattern swap is watched

Every pattern A ↔ B swap is animated, never cut. The 3-step pattern (from Grammar 1's join-the-sentences shape, reused here for the swap):

```
Pattern A: Bellingham gave the trophy to Xabi Alonso.
                                       ^^^                 ↓ Step 1: tap to GRAB the indirect object ("to Xabi Alonso")
Bellingham gave the trophy [to Xabi Alonso ↑]
                                                            ↓ Step 2: tap to MOVE it before the direct object
Bellingham gave Xabi Alonso the trophy.
                                                            ↓ Step 3: tap to STRIP the "to"
Pattern B: Bellingham gave Xabi Alonso the trophy. ✅
```

Tricky case shown wrong-first on s17: putting "to" before the pronoun in Pattern B (e.g. *gave to me the trophy* — wrong). Red shake → re-position → correct.

## JS safety contract (locked)

- ONE `<script>` block at the bottom.
- All sentence content in **backtick template strings** OR **HTML entities** (`&apos;`).
