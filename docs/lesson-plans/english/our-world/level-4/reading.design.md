## The spine — Leo's First Geocache Hunt

Leo is going on his first geocache hunt at the foot of Mount Fuji. A **5-step flowchart** sits on the deck from slide 3 with all 5 slots empty. As each paragraph + comprehension check completes during Read Together, one slot fills. The **compass interactive** (s6) is Leo's navigation tool — the same UI returns in the Think Aloud beat to do the LP's "name a country north of China" task. Practice Activity 2 lets him put the 5 cache-discovery steps from ¶3 into the canonical 1-2-3-4-5 order. Payoff at slide 42: the cache is **FOUND**, opened, and Leo's treasure (his choice of a soccer card or Marvel mini-figure) is revealed alongside the captioned 5-step flowchart.

**Why this clears the two bars:**

- *Load-bearing:* the flowchart literally cannot fill unless Leo reads each paragraph and answers its comprehension check. The compass is the only tool that solves the Think Aloud direction task. Practice Activity 2 requires using *first/next/then/finally* signal words to order steps — no shortcut.
- *No leaps / no ambush:* every content word is taught BEFORE Leo meets it in the passage. *hiders, seekers, treasure, ornament, sinking, compass* are mini-gamed on s5-s9. *outdoor, app, clues* are inline first-encounter glosses on their respective paragraphs (faster, since they're guessable).
- *Accumulates:* the 5-slot flowchart grows visibly across slides 13-28; the compass returns at s29; the strategy signal words taught on s32-33 are the literal tools Leo uses on s34-36.
- *Earned payoff:* the cache opens only after all 5 flowchart steps + all 4 paragraphs + the strategy practice are done. The "treasure" Leo finds is shaped by his Extend-beat hobby choice.
- *Threads LP beats verbatim:* every LP item appears — the act-it-out warm-up with photos as the signal-word demo, the predict/explain pre-read, ¶-by-¶ comprehension Qs (LP-verbatim wording), the compass graphic-literacy script, the Weird-But-True message-in-a-bottle, Activity 2's 5-step ordering with LP-verbatim answer key, Activity 3's 8-photo label task, Activity 4's importance sort, the Extend hobby builder, the Recap script, and the formative bug-collecting 3-step order.

## The passage (TR 8.7 verbatim)

```
HIDE and SEEK

Geocaching is a fun outdoor hobby. It's like a game of hide and seek,
with the hiders leaving clues for the seekers to find. Here's how it works:
The hiders hide a box, or cache. Inside the box, they put some "treasure"
— small objects for you to find — and a notebook. If you want to be a
seeker, or geocacher, you use an app that tells you where there are caches
near you.

Then you look for the cache. To help you find it, you need to have your
smartphone with online clues, or a map and compass. You should also take
a pen or pencil and a few small treasures to leave for other people.

When you find the cache, you look for the notebook inside. Then you write
your name and the date. Then you look at the treasures in the box, and
choose something you like to take away. You might choose a small toy, an
ornament, or a little book. Don't forget to leave another treasure in
its place!

Geocaching is a great way to explore new places and make new friends.
Many geocachers also help to clean up the places they visit by picking up
garbage they see along the way.

One geocacher says it's a great hobby because "It gets the whole family
outside and doing something fun together. I love it!"

[Weird but True] A Japanese sailor sent a message in a bottle when his
boat was sinking. The message asked for help. About 150 years later, the
bottle arrived in the town where he was born!
```

## The 5 cache-discovery steps (LP Activity 2 — canonical order)

1. **You find the cache and open it.**
2. **You look for the notebook.**
3. **You write your name and the date.**
4. **You choose a treasure to take away.**
5. **You leave an object in the box for someone else to find.**

This is the order Leo must produce in Practice (s34-35). Each step is also the caption text that lands on the spine flowchart slot at the payoff.
