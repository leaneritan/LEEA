🐚 ABOUT THE PHOTO — Ammonite Fossils
🐚
This photo shows ammonite fossils. Ammonites looked like squids and lived inside ring-shaped shells.
📅
These creatures first appeared about 240 million years ago. They lived in large groups called schools.
🦖
They became extinct with the dinosaurs, about 65 million years ago.
🔬
Ammonite fossils are some of the most common fossils found today. Scientists use their shapes and sizes to tell how old other fossils are.
🔬 MEET THE EXPLORER — Jørn Hurum
👨‍🔬
Jørn Hurum — Paleontologist & National Geographic Explorer. He collects and studies fossils buried for millions of years.
🏔️
Each August, he leads a team to Norway, north of the Arctic Circle. For ~3 weeks, there is little snow and lots of exposed rock — perfect for finding fossils.
🦕
Hurum's team found about 60 sea-reptile skeletons. One famous discovery, Predator X, is a 50-foot pliosaur that hunted in the cold ocean.
💬 THE EXPLORER'S WORDS
"As a child, I felt very alone with my interest in fossils. Finally, at age 13, I discovered there was a museum in Norway that actually employed people to study fossils."
— Jørn Hurum, Paleontologist, National Geographic Explorer
💡 Jørn was your age when he found out his hobby could be a real job. Your hobbies matter too!
