📐 What is "Explanation" writing?
When you write an explanation, you describe something in general. You explain what it is and how you do it. You also define difficult words and give examples. Use words like for example and such as.

¶2 — The DEFINITION 🏷️
The word "origami" comes from two Japanese words: "ori," which means "🌀 folding", and "Kami," which means "paper." In origami, you always begin with one square sheet of paper. Then you fold the paper many times to make a shape.
Q3. What does "origami" literally mean (definition)?
Q4. What shape do you ALWAYS begin with?
📐 Notice the definition shape: WORD + which means + MEANING (ori → folding · kami → paper).
¶3 — The EXAMPLES 🔍
Origami is very creative. You can make simple shapes, for example, a little bug. Or you can make a difficult 🎨 design. To make a difficult shape, such as a 🐲 dragon, you have to fold the paper many times in different ways. The most famous origami design is the Japanese paper 🕊️ crane. Origami isn't always easy, but it's fun!

📐 The 4 STEPS of Explanation writing
💬 Dad: Every Explanation paragraph does FOUR things. Memorize this — you'll use it for YOUR hobby.
1
NAME the hobby
"Origami is a Japanese art..."
2
DEFINE what it is
"...folding (ori) paper (kami) into shapes."
3
EXPLAIN how you do it
"Begin with one square sheet. Then you fold..."
4
GIVE examples
"For example, a little bug. Such as a dragon."

⚠️ Be the Expert — its vs it's
💬 Dad: 90% of writers mix these up. The test: try replacing the word with it is. If the sentence still makes sense → use it's. If not → use its.
🏷️ its (no apostrophe · possessive)
Belongs to it. The dog wags its tail.
🤝 it's (= it is · contraction)
It's = it is. It's fun when the dog chases its own tail.
1. The cat is licking paw.
2. raining outside.
3. The team won first game.
