📐 What is Explanation Writing?
When you write an explanation, you describe something in general. You explain what it is and how you do it. You also define difficult words and give examples. Use words like for example and such as.
💬 Dad: Today we're writing an explanation about a HOBBY.
⚽ Leo: Can I write about soccer or my Marvel comics?
💬 Dad: Yes! Pick any hobby YOU love. The plan is the same.

¶1 — When & where
Fun with Paper
The Japanese invented 📜 origami in the 17th century, and now it is popular everywhere in the world.
Q1. WHO invented origami?
Q2. WHEN?
¶2 — The DEFINITION 🏷️
The word "origami" comes from two Japanese words: "ori," which means "🌀 folding", and "Kami," which means "paper." In origami, you always begin with one square sheet of paper. Then you fold the paper many times to make a shape.
Q3. What does "origami" literally mean?
Q4. What shape do you ALWAYS begin with?
📐 Definition shape: WORD + which means + MEANING (ori → folding · kami → paper).
¶3 — The EXAMPLES 🔍
Origami is very creative. You can make simple shapes, for example, a little bug. Or you can make a difficult 🎨 design. To make a difficult shape, such as a 🐲 dragon, you have to fold the paper many times in different ways. The most famous origami design is the Japanese paper 🕊️ crane. Origami isn't always easy, but it's fun!

📐 The 4 STEPS of Explanation Writing
💬
Every Explanation paragraph does FOUR things. Memorize this — you'll use it for YOUR hobby.
1
NAME the hobby
"Origami is a Japanese art..."
2
DEFINE what it is
"...folding (ori) paper (kami) into shapes."
3
EXPLAIN how you do it
"Begin with one square sheet. Then you fold..."
4
GIVE examples
"For example, a little bug. Such as a dragon."
🗂️ The 4 steps ARE the 4 columns of the chart you'll fill on the Plan tab.

Compare two intros. Find what they have in common — that's the FORMULA every good intro uses.
Intro (a) — Fun with Paper (ORIGAMI)
"The Japanese invented origami in the 17th century, and now it is popular everywhere in the world."
Intro (b) — Hide and Seek (GEOCACHING · SB p.140)
"Geocaching is a fun outdoor hobby. It's like a game of hide and seek, with the hiders leaving clues for the seekers to find."
Q1. What do the two intros have in COMMON?
Q2. Which intro tells WHEN the hobby started?
Q3. Which intro uses a COMPARISON (it's LIKE another game)?
