Tricky! WHO goes in the MIDDLE
PL: "Does this sentence make sense?"
Watch out
SOURCE
My friend knows a lot about actors. She collects movie posters.
❌ Direct swap: My friend knows a lot about actors who collects movie posters.
→ This says the ACTORS collect movie posters. That's not right!
The fix: Put "who" right next to "my friend".
✅ My friend who collects movie posters knows a lot about actors.

Grammar Detective — Tap the Mistake
Each sentence has ONE wrong word
5 cases
CASE 1
I have a ball who is round.
✅ Right! A ball is a thing — use "that" or "which", not "who".
CASE 2
Haaland is the striker which plays for Man City.
✅ Right! Haaland is a PERSON — use "who".
CASE 3
I have a cousin who she likes Avatar.
✅ Right! "who" REPLACES "she" — don't keep both.
CASE 4
The stadium who holds 90,000 fans is Camp Nou.
✅ Right! A stadium is a thing — use "that" or "which".
CASE 5
I know three people who collects fossils.
✅ Right! "Three people" is plural → "who collect" (no s).
CASE 6
My brother is a good swimmer which loves diving.
✅ Right! "Swimmer" is a person → use who, not which.
(Workbook p. 34 transform pair)
CASE 7
Bai is an creative person who has a great imagination.
✅ Right! Use "a creative" — "an" is only before vowel sounds. (creative starts with /k/)
(Workbook p. 34, Exercise 2.4)
CASE 8 · Tricky!
The boy who are competing in the contest are good.
✅ Right! Here who isn't needed — the verb (are) already shows it's about the boys. (Workbook p. 34, Exercise 1: who/X rule.) Better: "The boys competing in the contest are good."
