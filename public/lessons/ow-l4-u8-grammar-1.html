Unit 8·Tricky — WHO in the Middle📖 LP p.269
⚠️ Tricky! WHO must go right after the PERSON
💬 Dad: "Read both sentences carefully. My friend knows a lot about actors. She collects movie posters. Who collects movie posters — my friend or the actors?"
FIRST ATTEMPT — wrong!
My friend knows a lot about actors who collects movie posters.
❌ This says actors collect movie posters — but it's my friend who collects them! And actors is plural, so the verb would be collect, not collects. Two problems in one!
CORRECT — who in the MIDDLE!
✅ My friend who collects movie posters knows a lot about actors.
📐 RULE: who + verb phrase goes RIGHT AFTER the PERSON we're describing — even if it lands in the middle of the sentence!

Unit 8·Error Spotter — find the mistake🎓 Grammar in Depth
🔍 Error Spotter — Find the Mistake!
💬 Dad: "Tap the WRONG part in each sentence. All four sentences are about PEOPLE — that's why we use who."
E1: "I have a cousin who she collects coins."
(tap the wrong word)
E2: "I know three people who collects fossils."
(plural subject = no -s)
E3: "My friend knows a lot about actors who collects movie posters."
(wrong position — should follow "my friend")
E4: "Bellingham is a player he plays at the Bernabéu."
(missing "who" — and "he" is the wrong word)
📐 Three error types to watch: double pronoun (who he), wrong position (clause not next to the person), wrong verb match (plural with -s).

Unit 8·What We Learned Today📖 Summary
🎓 Three Rules to Remember
📐
RULE 1 — The Pattern
PERSON + who + verb phrase
"a brother who loves video games"
🔄
RULE 2 — WHO never changes
Boy or girl, one or many — it's always who.
He → who · She → who · They → who
👤
RULE 3 — PEOPLE ONLY
Today we used who only for people. Things use other words later.
no which · no that
📐 Verb match: ONE person → verb + -s · MANY people → verb (no -s).
