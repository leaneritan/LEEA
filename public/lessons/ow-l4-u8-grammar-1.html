clause
Noun · Academic
DEFINITION
A part of a sentence that has a verb in it. Today's "who clause" is a small clause about a person.
"A photographer is a person who takes photos." — "who takes photos" is a who-clause.

PHASE 5 · 6 MIN
🧠

Grammar in Depth

When to use who vs which/that. Common mistakes. How to turn two sentences into one.

🧭
When to Use WHO
Only for people — never for things
Rule
WHO
for PEOPLE 👤
Haaland who plays for Man City
My cousin who lives in Osaka
Batman who fights crime
WHICH / THAT
for THINGS 📦 and ANIMALS 🐾
The ball that is round
The stadium which is huge
The dog that barks
⚠️
Common Mistakes
Don't use who for things!
Watch out
❌
I have a ball who is round.
✅
I have a ball that is round.
❌
The stadium who is big.
✅
The stadium which is big.
🔄
Two Sentences → One
Replace the repeated person with who
Transform
Vinicius is a player. Vinicius is fast.
→
Vinicius is a player who is fast.
Salah scores goals. Salah plays for Liverpool.
→
Salah, who scores goals, plays for Liverpool.
Batman is a hero. Batman protects Gotham.
→
Batman is a hero who protects Gotham.
🚀
