clause
Noun · Academic
DEFINITION
A part of a sentence that has a verb inside it. Today's "who-clause" is a small clause about a person.
"Haaland is the striker who plays for Man City." — "who plays for Man City" is a who-clause!
Leo — count the verbs in a sentence and you find the clauses. Today's grammar adds a who-clause.

contraction
Noun · Academic
DEFINITION
Two words squeezed into one with an apostrophe (').
she's = she + is · who's = who + is · they're = they + are
Item 4 today says: "I have an aunt who's very creative." That apostrophe in who's = "who" + "is"!

Tricky! WHO goes in the MIDDLE
PL Present · "Does this sentence make sense?"
Watch out
SOURCE
My friend knows a lot about actors. She collects movie posters.
❌ If we just swap "She" with "who":
My friend knows a lot about actors who collects movie posters.
→ This says the ACTORS collect movie posters. That's not right!
The fix: Put "who" right next to "my friend" — so the clause describes the friend, not the actors.
✅ My friend who collects movie posters knows a lot about actors.
In this case, who comes in the middle of the sentence — right after "my friend".

Grammar in Depth

What's a clause, really? When does who go in the middle vs the end? A T-chart sort to nail it down.

🧠
What is a Who-Clause?
A small piece with a verb that describes the person
In Depth
A WHO-CLAUSE HAS 2 PARTS
🔗
who
(the link)
🎯
verb phrase
(what the person does)
Haaland is the striker who scores goals for Man City.
Notice: every who-clause has a verb in it ("scores", "plays", "collects"). That verb does the describing.
🗂️
Sort: WHO or WHICH/THAT?
Tap each chip → it goes to the right column
Sort
WHO 👤
for PEOPLE
WHICH / THAT 📦
for THINGS & ANIMALS
📍
Where Does WHO Go?
Right after the person you're describing
Position
Haaland ⬅ who scores for Man City is dangerous.
I love the player ⬅ who wears number 9.
Cousins ⬅ who play in a musical group have fun.
Rule: "who-clause" sticks to the person like glue. Don't put anything between them.
