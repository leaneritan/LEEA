About the Photo · Meet the Explorer
The photo on p. 143 shows ammonite fossils. Ammonites looked like squids inside ring-shaped shells, lived in groups called "schools," and went extinct with the dinosaurs about 65 million years ago. Scientists use ammonite shells to tell how old OTHER fossils are. Ask Leo: "Have you ever found a rock that looked like an animal?"

About the Photo 📸

🐚🦴
Ammonite fossils — ring-shaped
shells of an extinct sea creature
⏳ WHEN: 240 million years ago
Ammonites went extinct with the dinosaurs ~65 million years ago 🦖
What they were: Ammonites looked like squids 🦑 living inside hard, ring-shaped shells. They lived in big groups called "schools."
Where you find them: Ammonite fossils are some of the most common fossils on Earth. Anywhere with old sea rocks — beaches, mountains that used to be ocean.
Why scientists love them: Different shaped shells appeared at different times. So if you find an ammonite, you can tell how OLD the rocks around it are! 🗓️🪨
Dad 💬 "This is why Hurum hunts for fossils every August — they tell a 240-million-year story!"
Leo 💬 "Like collecting Pokémon — but the cards are 240 million years old!"
