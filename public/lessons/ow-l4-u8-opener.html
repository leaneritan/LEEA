/ˈkæpʃən/ · cap·tion · noun
💬 Dad: "Every magazine photo has a caption — a short sentence that explains the photo. Which caption below is the BEST? Leo, you choose first!"
🐻‍❄️📷❄️ ✓ Perfect caption!

A caption is a short sentence under a photo that tells who or what is in the photo, and what is happening.
🗒️ Great captions are: Specific (who/what/where) · Short (1–2 sentences) · Interesting (vivid adjectives!)

🗣️ Discussion Questions
Tap each card to reveal the model answer — but ask Leo first!
What do you see in the photo?
A polar bear, a camera, and ice/snow. The bear is very close to the camera.
What does the polar bear seem to be doing?
The bear is investigating the camera — sniffing it or pawing at it. It looks curious!
Where is the person who owns the camera?
Probably far away — it's dangerous to be close to a polar bear! Everyone backed away for safety.
How do you think the photographer felt?
Excited! And maybe a little scared. But happy — this is an incredible, once-in-a-lifetime photo!

BE THE EXPERT — About the Photo
Background knowledge for Dad to share with Leo
Be the Expert
About the Photo
🐻‍❄️📷
Photographer: Roie Galitz
Location: Svalbard / Greenland, Arctic
Context: Wildlife photography workshop
Bear weight: ~500 kg
What Happened
This photo shows a male polar bear who came across a group of people at a wildlife photography workshop. As the curious bear came closer, everyone backed away for safety. The polar bear then investigated the camera equipment — apparently interested in joining the workshop!
The Location
Svalbard/Greenland — deep in the Arctic between the North Atlantic and Arctic Oceans. About 80% of Greenland is covered in ice. Wildlife here includes polar bears, seals, whales, musk oxen, and sea eagles.
Discussion Prompts
🐾 "Why do you think the photographer set up his camera and stepped away?"
🐾 "Is wildlife photography a dangerous hobby? Why?"
🐾 "Would you like to photograph polar bears?"
