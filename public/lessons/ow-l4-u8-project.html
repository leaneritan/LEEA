Research your hobby
Research
Find information about your hobby. Use the name of the hobby or interest as a key word. You can also use words like how to. Use the text and photos from the unit, too.
Take notes as you research:
📝
What is it? What are the details?
Write short notes — not full sentences yet.
📝
How do you do it? What are the steps?
Think: First… Then… Finally…
📝
What drawings, photos, or objects can you show?
You can also bring objects to show.
📋
Prepare Your Report
SB p. 144 · 3 parts
Plan
1
Choose one of your favorite hobbies or interests.
2
Prepare a report for the class:
a. Explain details about it.
b. Explain how you do it.
c. Use drawings or photos. You can also bring objects to show.
Solo adaptation: Leo prepares the report. Dad helps with research and gives feedback.
📝
Report Template
What to include
Template
Your report should have 3 parts:
A
Details — What is the hobby? Where does it come from? Why is it interesting?
B
How — How do you do it? What steps do you follow? What do you need?
C
Visuals — Show drawings, photos, or objects. Explain what they show.
"My hobby is ___. It is interesting because ___. To do it, you need ___. First, you ___. Here is a picture of ___."
🗣️

Present your report
Present
Leo: Present your hobby report to Dad. Remember to speak slowly and clearly. Look at Dad when you speak!
Your presentation:
1
Name the hobby and give details about what it is.
2
Explain how you do it.
3
Show and talk about your drawings, photos, or objects.

Questions & feedback
Q&A
Dad: Ask Leo questions about his report. Use these sentence frames:
"What things does a person need to ___ ?"
"How did you learn about this hobby?"
"What do you like most about it?"
Teaching Tip
💡
Help Leo improve his oral presentation skills. Tell him to look at you when speaking, to speak slowly and clearly, and to speak loudly enough.
