Before You Read
Activate Prior Knowledge
Activate
Teacher: Tell students about hobbies that you had as a child. Say whether or not you still do them.
Ask the class:
🎨
What’s your favorite hobby?
💼
Do you think you could do this hobby as a job when you grow up? Why, or why not?
Let several students share before moving on.

After You Read
Review your questions and answers
Review
Instructions: Look at the questions in the first column of your chart. For each question, ask: “Was that question answered in the text? What was the answer?” Have students write responses in the right-hand column.
1️⃣
Read each question from the left column.
2️⃣
Ask: “Was this answered in the text?”
3️⃣
Write the answer in the right column.
Be the Expert — Reading Strategy: Ask Questions
During reading: Asking questions is a way of engaging with the text. When students are interested, their questions show they want to know more.
About unfamiliar words: Asking questions about unfamiliar words or difficult paragraphs can help students check their understanding.
After reading: Asking questions after reading can help students confirm their understanding.
