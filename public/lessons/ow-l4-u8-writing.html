Unit 8·¶3 — Find FOR EXAMPLE + SUCH AS📖 SB p.142 verbatim
🔍 Find the EXAMPLE phrases — for example · such as
Origami is very creative. You can make simple shapes, for example, a little bug. Or you can make a difficult 🎨 design. To make a difficult shape, such as a 🐲 dragon, you have to fold the paper many times in different ways. The most famous origami design is the Japanese paper 🕊️ crane. Origami isn't always easy, but it's fun!
🔍 Marker 1 — "for example"
Used to introduce ONE example.
simple shapes, for example, a little bug 🐞
🔍 Marker 2 — "such as"
Used to introduce examples too (just before the noun).
a difficult shape, such as a dragon 🐲
💡 LP rule: Use for example and such as to give specific examples. Without them, your writing has no examples!

Unit 8·4 Steps of Explanation Writing📐 Recap
📐 Every Explanation does 4 things
🎯
1. NAME the hobby
"Origami is a Japanese art..."
🏷️
2. DEFINE what it is
"...folding (ori) paper (kami) into shapes."
🛠️
3. EXPLAIN how you do it
"You begin with one square sheet. Then you fold..."
🔍
4. GIVE examples
"For example, a little bug. Such as a dragon."

Unit 8·Be the Expert — its vs it's📖 LP Writing Support
⚠️ Be the Expert — its vs it's
💬 Dad: "These two are EASY to mix up. The trick: if you can replace it with it is, use the apostrophe. If not, no apostrophe."
🏷️
its
NO apostrophe · possessive
Belongs to it.
The dog wags its tail.
(the tail belongs to the dog)
🤝
it's
WITH apostrophe · contraction
= it is
It's funny when the dog chases its own tail.
(It is funny... the tail belongs to the dog)
💡 TEST: Try replacing the word with "it is." If the sentence still makes sense, use it's. If not, use its.

Unit 8·Edit — Bonus: its / it's📖 LP Writing Support
⚠️ Bonus Edit — re-read for its / it's
💬 Dad: "If you wrote either word in your paragraph, do the test now: try replacing it with it is. If the sentence still makes sense → use it's. If not → use its."
🏷️ its (no apostrophe)
⚽ The soccer ball lost its air. (the air belongs to the ball)
🤝 it's (= it is)
⚽ It's fun to play soccer. (= It is fun to play soccer.)
📐 The Test (always works):
  1. Find every its or it's in your paragraph.
  2. Try replacing it with "it is."
  3. If the sentence STILL makes sense → use it's.
  4. If the sentence sounds WEIRD → use its.
🎯 90% of writers mix these up — Leo can be the expert!

Unit 8·What We Learned — 3 Big Rules🎯 Recap
📚 3 Big Rules — Explanation Writing
🗂️
Rule 1 — PLAN first
Fill the 4-column chart BEFORE you write. Phrases first, sentences later.
📐
Rule 2 — Do all 4 steps
NAME · DEFINE · EXPLAIN HOW · GIVE EXAMPLES. Miss one → readers get confused.
🔍
Rule 3 — Use example markers
Always include for example or such as. They turn vague writing into clear writing.
🎯 Bonus: master its (possessive) vs it's (= it is) — most adult writers still mix these up!
