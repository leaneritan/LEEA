Unit 9·Preteach — Pattern A: the more..., the more...🎧 TR 9.4
📐 Pattern A — two things increase together
THE PATTERN
The more + S + V, the more + S + V
💬 Dad: "One skater pulls a lot. The other skater spins a lot. Watch them join with the more..., the more...!"
WATCH IT BUILD
One skater pulls a lot. The other skater spins a lot.
📐 Both halves start with the more, and a comma always separates them.
Unit 9·Preteach — Pattern B: the more..., the -er...🎧 TR 9.4
📐 Pattern B — a comparative adjective for the effect
THE PATTERN
The more + S + V, comparative adj. + S + V
💬 Dad: "You push the swing. It goes fast. This time the second half uses a comparative word like faster instead of repeating the more!"
WATCH IT BUILD
You push the swing. It goes fast.
📐 The adjective changes shape: fast → the faster, good → the better, high → the higher.

Unit 9·Grammar in Depth — Cause → Effect📖 Be the Expert, p.153
🔬 Be the Expert — Grammar in Depth
In English, we can use a double comparative to show that one action causes another. Note that a comma always separates the two parts of the sentence.
CAUSE (first half)
One skater pulls.
→
EFFECT (second half)
The other spins.
✅ The more one skater pulls, the more the other skater spins.

Unit 9·Be the Expert — Our World in Context📖 SB p.153
⚡ Exercise Equipment and Friction
Some exercise equipment today can turn people's movements into electricity and energy. People ride on bikes or use machines and create friction. The more force they use, the more friction they create. Some exercise equipment can change the energy created by friction into electricity. Some gyms take the energy created by the equipment and use it to power the gym's lights and machines.
💡 A real double comparative in science: the more force, the more friction — and that friction can even make electricity!
Unit 9·Be the Expert — Our World in Context📖 SB p.153
🛝 A Seesaw is a Lever
A seesaw works like a lever. Levers allow people to lift objects or people that they would normally be unable to lift. The farther a person moves from the center of the seesaw, the more the lever helps lift the person on the other end. To maintain balance on a seesaw, a heavier person should sit closer to the center of the seesaw, and a lighter person should sit closer to the end.
💡 Another double comparative in science: the farther from the center, the more the lever helps.

Unit 9·What We Learned Today📖 Summary
🎓 Three Rules to Remember
📐
RULE 1 — Two Patterns
the more..., the more...
OR
the more..., the -er...
"the more you practice, the faster you run"
📍
RULE 2 — The Comma
A comma always separates the two halves — cause, then effect.
cause , effect
🔗
RULE 3 — Cause Then Effect
The first half always causes the second half to happen.
One skater pulls → the other spins
