BE THE EXPERT — About the Photo
Background knowledge for Dad to share with Leo
Be the Expert
About the Photo
🚵🌲
Activity: Mountain biking
Safety gear: Helmet & knee pads
Forces at play: gravity, direction, balance, lean
What's Happening
A mountain biker rides down a bumpy, hilly trail. He has to choose a direction and lean his body to turn — the same way a cyclist leans left to turn left. Gravity pulls him down the hill, so he has to control his speed carefully.
The Science
Mountain biking uses several forces at once: gravity pulls the rider downhill, friction from the brakes slows the bike down, and balance keeps the rider from falling over. That's why a helmet and knee pads matter — if a force makes him fall, the gear protects his body!
Discussion Prompts
⚡ "What forces do you feel when you ride down a hill fast?"
⚡ "Why do mountain bikers wear helmets and knee pads?"
⚡ "Would you like to try mountain biking?"

Teaching Tip — Feel the Force First
Teach force vocabulary through physical actions before naming them. Have Leo push a chair, then pull it back. Ask: "What did you just do to the chair?" Let him describe the action before you give the word "push" or "pull." This builds understanding through experience, not just memorisation.
👐
Act it out first
Push and pull real objects before naming the words — Leo discovers the meaning through action
💬
Use it in sentences
Model sentences with the new word first, then ask Leo to use it — "What happens when YOU push it?"
⚽
Connect to soccer
Ask: "What force do you use when you kick a soccer ball?" — Leo's favorite sport makes force vocabulary stick
